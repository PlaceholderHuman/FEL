UHM's Free-Electron Laser (FEL)


About Us

Summary

A free-electron laser is a powerful radiation amplifying tool with many different applications. For example, it can be used as a research tool for studying on a molecular level, with a wide range of tunable wavelengths. John Madey’s Mark III FEL is utilized within the university’s free-electron laser inverse-Compton interaction (FELICIA) configuration.

Current Goals

Because the FEL has been inoperative since 2016, several vacuum leaks have been sprung, and parts have worn from time. Currently, we are working to fix four different vacuum leaks. A new electron gun (thermionic) has already been produced.
